Me, Notes and books

Thanks to the Internet, I taught myself some algebra and geometry from notes and videos online. When I first studied algebraic geometry myself without knowing category theory and commutative algebra, I can hardly understand what is going on. I spended and wasted too much time struglling against many unproven theorems from commutative algebra and descriptions that seemed vague to me (like universal proerpties from category theory), until I started to study commutative algebra systematically from notes online (For instance, Gathmann's commutative algebra notes and introduction of category theory from FOAG). Learning videos (continue to update)

Here is a selected list of recommended videos which I watched before, to help someone dosen't know how to find learning materials and resources like me:
Algebraic Topology (in Chinese)
Lecterer: Chen-Yu Chi
Description: It starts from complex chain and singular homology, using acyclic model theorem to prove several important theorems. The course spends majority of time to teach homology but only 6 classes for cohomology.

Algebraic Geometry
Lecturer: Lothar Göttsche
Description: It is an introduction course about affine and projective varieties without mentioning sheaves and schemes, including for example product of varieties, rational map, finite fiber, dimensions, completeness. The last lecture is a breaf introduction about divisors and Riemann-Roch Theorem.

Riemannian Manifolds
Lecturer: Willian P. Minicozzi
Description: It is an introduction course about Riemannianian manifolds, form Levi-Civita connection, geodesics, curvature to Morse index Theorem and some basics of geometric analysis.

ALgabraic Geometry in Covid-19
Lecturer: Ravi Vakil
Description: Unlike Lothar Göttsche's introduction course, it establish the notions and instinct of sheaves and schemes at very beginning. The language is also more categorical.

Symplectic Geometry
Lecturer: Ben Webster
Description: A breaf introduction of symplectic geometry, using Ana Cannas da Silva's Lectures on Symplectic Geometry as teaching materials.
