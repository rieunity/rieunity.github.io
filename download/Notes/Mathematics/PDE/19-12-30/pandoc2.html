Second-order Elliptic Equations

Define the partial differential operator LLL to be

Lu=−∑i,j=1naij(x)uxixj+∑i=1nbi(x)uxi+c(x)u.Lu=-\sum_{i,j=1}^{n}a^{ij}(x)u_{x_ix_j}+\sum_{i=1}^{n} b^{i}(x)u_{x_i}+c(x)u. Lu=−i,j=1∑n​aij(x)uxi​xj​​+i=1∑n​bi(x)uxi​​+c(x)u.

We say LLL is (uniformly) elliptic if there exists a constant θ>0\theta>0θ>0 such that
\begin{equation}\sum_{i,j=1}{n}a{ij}(x)\xi_i\xi_j\ge \theta \left| \xi \right|^2 \label{uec}
\end{equation}
for a.e. x∈Ux\in Ux∈U and all ξ∈Rn\xi \in \mathbb{R}^{n}ξ∈Rn.
